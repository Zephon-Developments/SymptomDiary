Symptom Diary User Requirements

# User Requirements
- I want to be easily able to log a numerical pain level from 0 to 10, with 0 being no pain and 10 being the worst pain imaginable on a daily basis.
- I want to be able to log multiple symptoms in addition to pain level, such as fatigue, nausea, headache, and others, with a simple interface that allows me to quickly select from common symptoms or add custom ones.
- I want to be able to add freeform notes to each daily entry, so I can provide additional context about my symptoms, such as what I was doing when they occurred, how long they lasted, and any potential triggers or relief factors.
- I want to be able to review my symptom history in a clear and organized way, such as a timeline with pain levels and symbol indicators for different symptoms, so I can easily identify patterns and trends over time.
- I want the app to be easy to use and navigate, with a clean and intuitive interface that allows me to quickly log my symptoms and review my history without any confusion or frustration.
- I want the app to generate a printable report of my symptom history that I can share with my healthcare provider, so they can better understand my condition and make informed decisions about my care.
- I want the app to be secure and protect my personal health information, with strong privacy controls and data encryption to ensure that my information is safe and confidential.

# Interface Requirements
- The app should have a simple and intuitive interface for logging symptoms, with clear options for selecting pain levels and symptoms, and a text field for adding freeform notes.
- The app should have a clear and organized interface for reviewing symptom history, such as a timeline with pain levels and symbol indicators for different symptoms, and the ability to filter and sort entries by date, symptom, or pain level.
- The app should have a clear and easy-to-use interface for generating and sharing reports, with options for selecting the date range and format of the report, and a simple way to share it with healthcare providers or export it for personal use.
- On opening the app, the user should be able to immediately log a new symptom entry or review their history with minimal navigation, ensuring that the most common actions are easily accessible from the home screen.
- There should be a way to add missed entries for previous days, allowing users to maintain a complete symptom history even if they forget to log symptoms on a given day.

# Security and Privacy Requirements
- The app should implement strong encryption for all stored data, ensuring that user information is protected from unauthorized access.
- The app should require the user to reauthenticate their phone login on launch, ensuring that only the authorized user can access the app and their symptom data.
- The app should exit if left unattended for a configurable amount of time, defaulting to 5 minutes. This ensures that if the user forgets to close the app, their data remains secure.
- The app should not store any data in plaintext, and all non-setting information should be encrypted.
